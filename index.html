Haptic Tele-operation

Democratizing Robot-Assisted Minimally Invasive Surgery Training.

6-DOF Feedback Hybrid Kinematics Custom FOC Driver
View Project

The Mission

The Problem

Robot-Assisted Minimally Invasive Surgery (RMIS) is hindered by a significant training gap. Commercial haptic simulators are prohibitively expensive and mechanically complex, limiting availability to well-funded institutions.

The Solution

Interforce is a low-cost, 6-Degree-of-Freedom (DOF) haptic interface designed to bridge this gap. By measuring forces applied by the robot’s manipulator and applying opposing counter-forces upon the user, the sensation of force-feedback is realized. Through the Interforce system, an operator is able to “feel” what forces the robot is applying, allowing for a more natural connection and a greater depth in control.

Prototype Phase: Initial Motor & Driver Test

Technical Architecture

Kinematics

Uses a hybrid architecture coupling a 3-DOF quasi-serial manipulator for translation with a decoupled 3-DOF spherical wrist.

Electronics

Custom-designed Field-Oriented Control (FOC) driver system based on the TMC9660 IC.

Impact

Designed to replicate the kinematics of industry-standard tools like the da Vinci Master Tool Manipulator at a fraction of the cost (~$2000) and with High-Fidelity Haptics.
